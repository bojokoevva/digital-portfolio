Sunrise General Hospital Data Platform

Designing a relational database and analytics layer for a fictional hospital, starting from a narrative case and ending with a PostgreSQL implementation ready for dashboards.

Healthcare PostgreSQL ERD & 3NF Analytics
ISM 4212 – Database Design & Administration
University of South Florida · Prof. Nasir Uddin

Context & goal

Sunrise General Hospital is a detailed narrative case describing admissions, physicians, departments, appointments, diagnoses, and billing. The original description is written in business language, with overlapping rules and many implicit assumptions about how the hospital actually works.

My goal in this project was to turn that narrative into a clean, relational data platform: a normalized PostgreSQL schema with constraints, realistic sample data, and views that are ready to support operational and analytical reporting through BI tools.

Role Data modeler, database designer, and implementer
Setting Individual course project (capstone-style)
Focus Schema design, constraints, seed data, analytics-ready views
Tools & tech PostgreSQL · DBeaver · ERDPlus · SQL · Tableau (planned)

Technical design

I began by extracting all entities, attributes, and business rules from the narrative, then designed an entity–relationship diagram and converted it into a 3NF relational schema with clear primary and foreign keys, plus business-driven constraints.

  • Modeled core entities such as Department, Doctor, Patient, InsurancePlan, Appointment, Diagnosis, Appointment_Diagnosis, and Billing, with relationships that reflect real hospital workflows.
  • Applied 3NF to separate patient demographics, encounters, diagnoses, and billing into distinct tables, reducing redundancy and improving consistency.
  • Defined a composite primary key in Appointment_Diagnosis so that each appointment can safely carry multiple diagnoses without duplicates.
  • Implemented integrity constraints such as valid appointment statuses and allowed values for patient sex, and enforced non-negative billing amounts.

Key logic & analytics

Once the schema was implemented in PostgreSQL, I focused on queries and sanity checks that prove the model behaves correctly and can support analytics on top of it.

  • Wrote sanity-check queries to count departments, doctors, patients, insurance plans, diagnoses, appointments, billing rows, and appointment–diagnosis pairs to validate referential integrity.
  • Built joins across Appointment, Doctor, Department, Patient, and Appointment_Diagnosis to verify that sample data returns the expected visits and diagnoses.
  • Designed query patterns that can be turned into dashboards: admissions by department, diagnoses per physician, and completed visits vs. scheduled/no-show appointments.
  • Ensured that each Billing row links to exactly one completed appointment, enabling accurate revenue and payer analysis.

Impact & what I learned

Even as a fictional case, Sunrise General Hospital feels close to real healthcare data challenges. Every sentence in the narrative had to be translated into a rule, relationship, or constraint in the schema.

  • Strengthened my ability to turn long, messy narratives into a clean, normalized schema without losing important business rules.
  • Practiced designing databases that support both transaction-level operations and analytics, instead of choosing one or the other.
  • Gained confidence in using PostgreSQL for constraint design, seed data, and sanity-check queries that catch mistakes early.

This project fits directly into my interest in healthcare data and in building trustworthy systems for high-stakes decision making around patients, staffing, and resources.
